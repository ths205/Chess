Attack types

Pin

A pin is created by attacking another piece such that if the attacked piece moves out of the way it would lead to the capture of a more valuable piece. There are two types of pins in chess are absolute pin and relative pin. The absolute pin is the piece behind the pinned piece is a king, then the pinned piece cannot move out of the way because it is not allowed to put one's own king in check. The relative pin is if the pinned piece does move out of its way leading to the capture of a more valuable piece.The picture below is an example.

Skewer

Skewers are similar to pins but they work in reverse. It is created when the more valuable piece is being attacked and has to move out of the way leaving open a less valuable piece to be captured.

Forks

A fork is created when a piece is able to attack two pieces at the same time. The piece that is more known to makes forks is the knight, but any piece can create a fork. There is also special type of fork called the royal fork. A royal fork is when a piece is able to attack the, king, queen, and the rook at the same time.
